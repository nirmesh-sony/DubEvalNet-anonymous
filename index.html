DubEvalNet: Can Machines Match Human Perception in AI Dubbing Evaluation?

Anonymous Submission

Anonymous

Quality assessment of AI-generated dubbed content is a complex and challenging perceptual task that requires evaluating various interconnected factors, including audio-visual synchronization, consistency between speaker and character, intelligibility, emotional congruence, and semantic coherence. Existing objective metrics treat these aspects separately and fail to reflect holistic human perception. In this paper, we introduce DubEvalNet, a multimodal scoring framework designed to predict overall dubbing quality. To this extent, we construct a large-scale Hindi-English dubbing corpus by translating dialogue-rich video datasets using state-of-the-art Neural Machine Translation (NMT) and Text to Speech Synthesis (TTS) systems. Human raters evaluate dubbed content across key perceptual dimensions, while weak labels are derived by aggregating multiple objective metrics through weighted fusion. Our model employs a multimodal fusion architecture with a regression head trained to align with subjective scores. Fine-tuning on a small set of human annotations yields a strong correlation (75\%+) with human evaluations. DubEvalNet is the first unified, scalable, and human-aligned framework for automatic perceptual evaluation of AI dubbing, addressing a key gap in multimodal content assessment.
